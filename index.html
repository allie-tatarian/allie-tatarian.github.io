About Me

Hi there! I'm an academic librarian who is passionate about open science. Before I became a librarian, I did microbiology research - my time on the bench inspired me to be active in the open science community. I think this background helps me understand the challenges researchers face and makes me even more committed to advancing open scholarship.

Right now, I work mostly in research data management at Tufts University's Hirsh Health Sciences Library. While my current role focuses on RDM, I'm still interested in other aspects of open science, like free and open source software, linked open data, and open access publishing and preprints. I even get to do a bit of preprint advocacy and FOSS peer review on the side, so I can stay connected to the broader open science community.

In my current role, I get to be involved in many aspects of research support. I consult with researchers on their data management plans, help them navigate metadata standards, and share best practices for data sharing. I really enjoy creating and leading workshops on these topics - it's always exciting to see researchers have those "aha!" moments when complex concepts click.

Recently, I've been diving into the world of AI literacy in research. I've had the opportunity to lead some engaging conference workshops on this topic and even created an online guide that's been getting a lot of attention from our research community. It's fascinating to explore how AI is changing the research landscape and to help researchers understand its potential and pitfalls.

Looking ahead, I'm excited about co-leading Tufts' Data Management Team. It's a great chance to work with people across departments and make a collective impact on how our institution handles research data.

If you're interested in chatting about research data, open science, or how we can work together to support the research community, I'd love to connect!
