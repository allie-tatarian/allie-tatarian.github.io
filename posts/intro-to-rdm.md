# Introduction to Research Data Management

Research Data Management (RDM) is a critical aspect of modern scientific research. It encompasses the organization, storage, preservation, and sharing of data collected and used in research projects.

## Why is RDM important?

1. **Reproducibility**: Good RDM practices ensure that research can be verified and reproduced by other scientists.
2. **Efficiency**: Well-managed data is easier to find and use, saving time and resources.
3. **Compliance**: Many funding agencies and journals now require data management plans and data sharing.

## Key components of RDM

- Data collection and documentation
- Data storage and backup
- Data sharing and preservation
- Data security and ethics

In future posts, we'll dive deeper into each of these components and discuss best practices for implementing them in your research workflow.
